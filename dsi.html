Department of Startups and Innovation

ADepartment of Startups and Innovation Panel

After a decade of existence of the E-cell, this year we came up with a new program to actually groom the startups in college undertaken by the Department of Startups and Innovation. We identified leaders in the industry in fields ranging from real estate to waste management to Venture Capitalists. A board was formed with 6 such mentors who will be guiding and spear heading the startup movement in college for the year 2019. After extensive surveys, form fillups and interviews, 20 student teams were selected for startup mentorship. Connecting Founders to Industry experts.

The Mentorship Program by the Department of Startups and innovation is an innovation program for in-house college startups. It provides startup enthusiasts early and easy access to relevant mentors. Through a theme based approach, the program is designed to equip young college entrepreneurs in nascent markets with the capability to create engagement and interact with real business mentors. Powered by a dedicated network of entrepreneurs and mentors, DSI brings together early stage college startups, industry leading companies, experts and mentors in a new format.

The above mentioned panel discussion had all the three stakeholders - the teams seeking mentorship, the mentors and the facilitators ( Department of Startups and Innovation) under one roof. The students explained the challenges/hurdles that they are facing preventing them to proceed with their idea. The mentors collectively analysed these difficulties and gave comprehensive suggestions and guidelines on how to go about their ideas.

Student perspective: The mentees who were seeking answers to their questions found it very valuable in terms of the insights they received from the expert mentors. They received actionable suggestions and clarity in terms of how to proceed with the nascent idea. They also expressed their interest in such future meetups.
Speaker perspective : The mentors were pleased to know that the ecell is taking efforts towards building a startup culture in college that goes beyond just hosting entrepreneurship related activities and events in college. They like the informal set up of the discussion and also expressed a desire to see more serious startups applying for mentorship.
Interview with Sudarshan Purohit: What do you think is the best way to promote startups? Encourage the students to take this seriously, help by providing some sort of bootstrapping so that they are incentivized to put their heart and soul into making the idea a reality.
